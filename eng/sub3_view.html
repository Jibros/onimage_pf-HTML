Park Hwan

Western Painting
Male, Visual Impairment

Park Hwan started his artwork right after completing his military service, and submitted his work to the Korean Art Exhibition and won his first prize.
3 weeks after finishing the Korea International Art Fair 2013, he came across a car accident. Being recognized as a unique painter, he had to face his fatal fate as a painter in his prime days. Total blindness that does not respond to light at all, he thought he could not draw again, but took the composition in his head, and started to cut and past threads rather than draw with pencils on the canvas.
He painted with his fingers, not with his brush to create colors. Acquaintances who saw his paintings introduced him to several galleries, but he did not show interest, however, one day a call from Gallery Coop changed his mind, and opened his first exhibition in 2017.
Although artist Park Hwan has lost his sight, he shows his strong will to work as a painter.
